## LOWI Terminology & Messaging Guardrails

These rules apply at all times when discussing or drafting LOWI content:

- Default to avoiding the word "lease" (or leasing, lease-to-own, leaseback) when describing LOWI's programs to government audiences; use "Deferred Payment Program," "DPP," "dry finance structure," or "pay-to-own arrangement" instead. This is primarily because certain governments (especially the USA) have legal restrictions on leases. However, "lease" is appropriate in two contexts: (1) when speaking to investors, where "aircraft lease" is the closest market analog and the understood framing, and (2) when actually structuring a traditional lease for a government that does not have lease restrictions.
- LOWI is the "financial integrator," not the "program integrator." LOWI arranges capital; the government operates, maintains, and commands the asset.
- The core thesis is "Borrowing from the Past, Not from the Future": mobilizing existing private capital (institutional investors, pension systems, sovereign wealth funds) rather than creating new tax or debt burdens.
- The "Dual Dividend" framework describes two benefits of private capital in defense: a Defense Dividend (citizens become active participants in national security, not passive taxpayers) and an Economic Dividend (return-oriented capital deploys faster and grows the economy rather than burdening it).
- DPP terms are deliberately shorter than the useful life of the asset; the goal is quick, efficient ownership transfer.
- Writing voice for LOWI substantive content: declarative, factual, analytical with edge. Avoid tricolons, effusive framing, and editorial commentary; these read as AI-generated. When in doubt, tighten.
